Petra

Petra, often referred to as the "Rose City" due to the pink-hued stone from which it is carved, is one of the most famous archaeological sites in the world. Located in southern Jordan, Petra was the capital of the Nabataean Kingdom around the 4th century BC. This ancient city is renowned for its intricate rock-cut architecture and water management system, which allowed the Nabataeans to thrive in the arid desert environment.

The most iconic structure in Petra is Al-Khazneh, or "The Treasury," a grand façade carved directly into the rock face that served as a royal tomb. Petra’s other notable landmarks include the Monastery (Ad Deir), the Siq (a narrow gorge that serves as the entrance to the city), and hundreds of tombs, temples, and dwellings scattered throughout the site.

Petra played a significant role as a bustling trade hub, linking the East and West through spice and silk trade routes. Today, it is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World. Millions of visitors come each year to explore its stunning, ancient architecture and learn about the history of this remarkable desert city.
